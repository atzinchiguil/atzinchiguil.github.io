Atzin Chiguil

I am a first-year Ph.D. student in Economics at Rice University. My research interests lie in macroeconomics, monetary economics, labor economics, and macroeconomic policy. I have worked on research projects related to the transmission of monetary policy, the effects of public investment on economic activity and employment, the impact of minimum wage increases on labor market transitions and aggregate employment, and tax preferences, among other topics.

Before joining the Ph.D. program at Rice University, I worked as an economist at Banco de México in the Governing Board, as an analyst at the Ministry of Finance, and most recently as a research assistant at El Colegio de México. I hold a B.A. in Economics from the National Autonomous University of Mexico and an M.A. in Economics from El Colegio de México.

Research interests: Macroeconomics, Monetary Economics, Labor Economics, Firm Dynamics, Macroeconomic Policy.
